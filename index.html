Workflow

How each module works.

Three tools, three workflows — each designed to eliminate manual steps.

S
SiteIQ — Site Screening
Module 01 · Live Now
Open in App →
1

Locate Your Site

Search by place name, paste Google Maps or lat/lon coordinates, or upload a KML/KMZ file. The satellite map centres on your site instantly.

2

Set Project Parameters

Enter project name, country, and estimated site area in hectares. SiteIQ fetches GHI, irradiation, terrain overview, flood risk, and regulatory flags automatically.

3

Download PDF Report

Get a client-ready PDF with solar resource data, monthly irradiation chart, terrain summary, and a go/no-go verdict. Ready for your next client meeting.

T
TopoIQ — Terrain Extraction
Module 02 · Live Now
Open in App →
1

Draw Your Boundary

Navigate to your site on the satellite map. Use the polygon tool to draw the exact site boundary — click to place vertices, double-click or hit Finish to close. Or upload a DXF/KML file.

2

Extract Terrain Data

TopoIQ fetches Copernicus GLO-30 satellite DEM for your PV Area, computes elevation profiles, slope heatmaps, and contour lines at your chosen interval automatically.

3

Export DXF & PDF

Download a DXF terrain file with contour lines at your chosen spacing — ready for detailed 3D study in any CAD software — plus a PDF terrain report with elevation and slope maps.

Y
YieldIQ — Energy Yield Estimation
Module 03 · Live Now
Open in App →
1

Enter Site & System Parameters

Paste coordinates or a Google Maps link. Select your mounting type (Fixed Tilt or Single-Axis Tracker), module wattage, system losses, and DC capacity in MWp.

2

Compare 4 Configurations

YieldIQ runs 1P Fixed, 2P Fixed, 1P Tracker, and 2P Tracker simultaneously using PVGIS JRC data. Results include specific yield, performance ratio, capacity factor, and GCR-based shading losses.

3

Download Yield Report

Get a PDF comparing all 4 configurations with monthly energy charts in MWh, tracker gain analysis, and a pre-layout disclaimer. Ready for internal review before committing to a full layout.
