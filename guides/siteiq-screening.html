Understanding SiteIQ screening

One report for early go/no-go — solar, terrain, flood flag, land use, and country-aware regulatory context.

What SiteIQ evaluates

  • Solar resource — GHI and configuration-aware yield proxies from PVGIS (JRC)
  • Terrain — sparse pin sampling or TopoIQ-confirmed GLO-30 when available
  • Flood indicator — elevation-based heuristic, not official flood mapping
  • Land use — Standard ground mount vs Agri-PV (dual use) density assumptions
  • Regulatory flags — country-aware checklist pointers (not legal advice)

PVMath Score

The PVMath Score (0–100) combines multiple screening categories so you can compare sites in a portfolio. It is a deterministic screening model for pre-feasibility — not a bankability or investment grade rating.

Category breakdowns appear in your PDF report and in the app detail panels.

Overall site verdict

The overall verdict summarizes combined findings into a plain-language recommendation tier. Use it with module-specific depth: run TopoIQ for terrain detail and YieldIQ for configuration-level energy comparison.

Capacity & land use

Indicative DC capacity uses industry-typical MWp/ha bands adjusted for land use (Standard vs Agri-PV) and mounting type (fixed tilt vs tracker), with a standard GCR band for screening. Layout-optimised designs may differ.

Flood and regulatory sections are indicators for your checklist. Confirm with local authorities, hydrology studies, and official datasets before investment.
