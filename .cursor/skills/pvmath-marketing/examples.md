# PVMath marketing — example outputs (gold standard)

Study tone and structure; do not copy verbatim for every post.

**Main**

TerrainIQ turns the same boundary into a screening-grade package: Copernicus GLO-30 terrain, 5 m layout grid, UTM LandXML, DXF contours with **SITE_BOUNDARY** linework, and US Survey Feet for US projects. Not survey-grade — but enough to test layout, talk to the client, and decide if the site earns LiDAR.

Screening-grade by design. Confirm critical grades before FEED.

Try it on your next boundary: siteiq.pvmath.com  
Guide: pvmath.com/guides/landxml-dxf-solar.html

#GroundMountSolar #SolarEPC #CivilEngineering

**Variation A (short)**

KMZ in. CAD needed now. LiDAR later.

TerrainIQ: GLO-30 terrain + parcel linework → LandXML/DXF (UTM, screening-grade). 5 m grid for layout; ~30 m native DEM — we say both.

siteiq.pvmath.com

**Variation B (technical)**

Parcel boundary → merged GLO-30 TIN (UTM WGS84) + contour DXF clipped to site polygon. Cross-row p95 available for tracker screening. Georef exports in metres or US Survey Feet; local-centroid package for origin-near layout.

Not a substitute for LiDAR or RTK. Suitable for pre-FEED go/no-go and CAD starter geometry.

## Anti-examples (never write like this)

- "Revolutionary AI-powered solar disruption"  
- "Guaranteed bankable yield"  
- "5 m LiDAR resolution" (false — 5 m is output grid, not sensor)  
- "Replace your civil engineer"  
- Publishing PVMath Score weights or verdict formulas
